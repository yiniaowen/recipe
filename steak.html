CHICKEN KATSU-STEAK RECIPE

Savor the irresistible crunch and savory flavors of our Chicken Katsu, a beloved Japanese classic that promises a mouthwatering experience. Tender, juicy chicken breasts are expertly breaded with a crisp, golden coating that crackles with every bite. The crispy, panko-crusted exterior provides a satisfying crunch, perfectly contrasting with the succulent, moist chicken inside.

Each piece of Chicken Katsu is seasoned to perfection and fried to achieve the perfect balance of flavors and textures. The result is a dish that's both comforting and delectable, with a delightful crunch that gives way to a tender, flavorful interior.

Accompanied by a bed of fluffy steamed rice and a side of fresh, crisp cabbage salad, our Chicken Katsu makes for a well-rounded and satisfying meal. Garnished with a sprinkle of sesame seeds and a wedge of lemon, this dish not only delights the taste buds but also pleases the eyes with its vibrant colors and appetizing presentation.

Whether you're enjoying it as a comforting weeknight dinner or serving it to impress at a special gathering, our Chicken Katsu is a delicious, crowd-pleasing choice that brings the best of Japanese cuisine to your table.

Ingredients

For the Chicken Katsu:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 ½ cups panko breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the Katsu Sauce:

  • ¼ cup ketchup
  • ¼ cup Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar

For Serving:

  • Cooked rice
  • Shredded cabbage
  • Lemon wedges

Intructions

1. Prepare the Chicken:

  • Pound the Chicken: Place chicken breasts between two sheets of plastic wrap. Use a meat mallet to gently pound them to an even thickness of about ½ inch.
  • Season and Dredge: Season chicken breasts with salt and pepper. Dredge each piece in flour, shaking off the excess. Dip into beaten eggs, then coat with panko breadcrumbs, pressing lightly to adhere.
  • Fry the Chicken: Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Fry the chicken for 4-5 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through. Drain on paper towels.

2. Make the Katsu Sauce:

  • Mix Ingredients: In a small bowl, whisk together ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, soy sauce, honey, and rice vinegar until smooth.

3. Serve:

  • Plate the Dish: Slice the cooked chicken into strips. Serve over cooked rice with shredded cabbage on the side. Drizzle with katsu sauce or serve on the side. Garnish with lemon wedges if desired.

Tips

  • For extra crunch, double-coat the chicken by dipping it in egg and breadcrumbs twice.
  • Serve with a side of miso soup or pickled vegetables for a complete meal.

And your delicious Chicken Katsu-Steak is ready!
