MATCHA CAKE RECIPE

Experience the delicate harmony of flavors with our exquisite Matcha Cake. This enchanting dessert combines the earthy essence of high-quality matcha green tea with the light, fluffy texture of a classic sponge cake. Each bite is a delightful journey, starting with the subtle bitterness of matcha, perfectly balanced by the sweetness of the cake and the creamy, rich frosting.

The cake layers are tender and moist, thanks to the addition of buttermilk, which adds a slight tang and enhances the overall flavor. The matcha-infused frosting is silky smooth, providing a luscious contrast to the airy cake. For an extra touch of elegance, we garnish the cake with a dusting of matcha powder and optional edible flowers or fresh berries, adding a pop of color and a hint of freshness.

Perfect for tea parties, birthdays, or as a unique gift, our Matcha Cake brings a touch of sophistication and a burst of flavor to any gathering. Embrace the tranquility and artistry of Japanese tea culture with this delectable dessert, and let the serene flavors of matcha transport you to a place of calm and delight.

For the Cake:

  • Â½ teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons matcha powder (high-quality)
  • Â½ cup unsalted butter (room temperature)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup buttermilk (or milk with 1 tablespoon lemon juice)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the Frosting:

  • 1 cup unsalted butter (room temperature)
  • 4 cups powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons matcha powder
  • 2-4 tablespoons heavy cream (or milk)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For Garnish (Optional):

  • Extra matcha powder for dusting
  • Edible flowers or fresh berries

Intructions

1. Prepare the Cake:

  • Preheat your oven to 350Â°F (175Â°C). Grease and flour two 8-inch round cake pans.
  • In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, and matcha powder.
  • In a large bowl, beat the butter until creamy using an electric mixer on medium speed. Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.
  • Add the dry ingredients to the butter mixture in three additions, alternating with the buttermilk, beginning and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix until just combined. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  • Divide the batter evenly between the prepared cake pans. Smooth the tops with a spatula.
  • Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Let the cakes cool in the pans for 10 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.

2. Prepare the Frosting:

  • In a large bowl, beat the butter until creamy using an electric mixer on medium speed.
  • Gradually add the powdered sugar, beating well after each addition. Add the matcha powder and beat until combined.
  • Add 2 tablespoons of heavy cream (or milk) and vanilla extract. Beat until smooth and spreadable. If the frosting is too thick, add more cream a tablespoon at a time until the desired consistency is reached.

3. Assemble the Cake:

  • Place one cake layer on a serving plate or cake stand. Spread a layer of frosting on top.
  • Place the second cake layer on top of the frosted layer. Spread frosting over the top and sides of the cake, smoothing it with a spatula.
  • For a decorative touch, dust the top with extra matcha powder and garnish with edible flowers or fresh berries if desired.

4. Serve:

  • Allow the cake to set for about 30 minutes before slicing to let the frosting firm up slightly.
  • Slice and serve. Enjoy the light, matcha-infused flavors!

Tips

  • For an extra boost of matcha flavor, you can mix a little matcha powder into the cake batter and frosting.
  • Make sure the butter and eggs are at room temperature for the best texture and consistency.
  • Store any leftover cake in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 3 days or in the refrigerator for up to 1 week.

And your delicious Matcha Cake is ready!
