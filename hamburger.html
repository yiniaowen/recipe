HAMBURGER STEAK RECIPE

Discover the savory delight of Japanese Hamburger Steak, a beloved classic that brings comfort and elegance to your plate. This dish features juicy, perfectly seasoned beef patties that are tender and flavorful, combining the rich taste of high-quality ground beef with a blend of aromatic spices and seasonings.

Each Hamburger Steak is pan-seared to golden perfection, developing a delicious caramelized crust that locks in all the savory juices. It is then simmered in a rich, umami-packed sauce made from a harmonious blend of soy sauce, mirin, and Worcestershire sauce, which adds a depth of flavor and a touch of sweetness. The result is a succulent, flavor-infused patty that's both satisfying and sophisticated.

The steak is typically served atop a bed of steamed rice or alongside fluffy mashed potatoes, making it the perfect base for the generous drizzle of the savory sauce. Garnished with a sprinkle of fresh herbs or a side of crisp, seasonal vegetables, Japanese Hamburger Steak offers a balanced meal that's as visually appealing as it is delicious.

Perfect for a cozy family dinner or a special occasion, this dish embodies the essence of Japanese home cooking, providing a comforting and indulgent experience that will leave you craving more. Ingredients

For the Hamburger Steak:

  • 1 lb (450g) ground pork
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil (for cooking)

For the Sauce:

  • 1/2 cup pork/chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch mixed with 1 tablespoon water (optional, for thickening)

Intructions

1. Prepare the Hamburger Steak:

  • In a large bowl, combine the ground pork, chopped onion, minced garlic, panko breadcrumbs, milk, egg, soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, salt, and pepper. Mix until just combined.
  • Divide the mixture into 4-6 portions and shape each portion into a patty.
  • Heat the vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat. Cook the patties for about 4-5 minutes on each side, or until cooked through and browned.

2. Prepare the Sauce:

  • In a small saucepan, combine the beef broth, soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, ketchup, and honey. Bring to a simmer over medium heat.
  • If desired, add the cornstarch mixture to the sauce and cook until it thickens to your liking.
  • Add 2 tablespoons of heavy cream (or milk) and vanilla extract. Beat until smooth and spreadable. If the frosting is too thick, add more cream a tablespoon at a time until the desired consistency is reached.

3. Serve:

  • Place the cooked patties on a serving plate. Spoon the sauce over the top.
  • Garnish with fresh parsley or green onions if desired. Serve with steamed rice and vegetables for a complete meal.

Tips

  • Add a teaspoon of sesame oil to the pork mixture for added depth of flavor.
  • You can prepare the patties ahead of time and freeze them. Cook from frozen, adding a few extra minutes to the cooking time..
  • Experiment with adding a splash of sake or mirin to the sauce for an authentic Japanese touch.

And your delicious Hamburger Steak is ready!
